## 2026-03-18
Game Design Ideas:



Room Music: create different audio tracks that can all work together. Fade channels in and out when transitioning rooms so there isn't an abrupt cut in the music, but each room can start to get its own identity. Thought comes from Luftrausers where each plane you'd build would get a different drum/bass/lead track so the music was always familiar but still unique.
Emergent Mechanics: I noticed slime will push the crate. We have the start of simulation mechanism. Add pressure plates as a simple activating trigger (think Minecraft redstone, but without wire tiles, just a simple pointer) to open doors, spawn enemies, drop loot, etc. People will make all sorts of Rube Goldberg machines when given the opportunity. I know you want to make multiplayer not simply ghosts - having two players need to stand each on different pressure plates to unlock a door means you can make Portal style puzzles.
Other Gameplay Needs: moving platforms, friendly NPCs, text on signs and NPCs, "reveal layer" (like foreground, but disapears when you're behind it, useful for hidden rooms). People can start building lore and telling stories. Leaving clues for other rooms.
Meta Gameplay Ideas: Locked rooms can only be accessed by traveling through neighbor rooms, not teleported into. Room reset button for when you leave and come back (right now the kill/collect counter resets, but the objects aren't there anymore creating a soft-lock on beating the room). Player's names in chat could also show their location so you can teleport to friends. Instant play link (some people might not spot the small Play button when they follow a direct link to a room). Star ratings and comments on rooms. Player levels or badges (could be simple xp for clearing rooms - maybe not ones created/modified by yourself - to encourage exploring other people's work and not farming your own easy to clear stuff as well as currated room selections. Maybe "seasons" or events with objectives and in-game rewards like special avatars). Marketplace for minted rooms that are looking for buyers. Player profiles show a list of what they've created.
Monetization ideas:
Premium assets: specific tilesets, objects, avatars, backgrounds, and music could all be sold to help rooms and players stand out.
UGC rev-share: community contributions could be free or premium. with the game taking a cut of sales. We could also link this to player profiles for credit.
Paying partners: could add their NFT project to give their holders special avatars. Or create premium avatars/assets for the partnership. Doodles themed levels, etc.
Room sales: should have a sale percentage fee (guessing they already do). You and I daily claiming terrirtory is like getting company stock.
Premium rooms: larger 2x2 (or whatever), special assets like boss enemies, maybe randomly scattered in unclaimed territory. Finding them could be a nice payday to the claimant if they are able to sell.
Tip system: like bits on Twitch, send a tip to a room builder who is doing cool stuff. Have a small fee on those transactions.
